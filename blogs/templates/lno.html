What is No/Low Code?

No/Low code platforms and tools enable non-technical users to create applications and software solutions with
pre-built components, visual interfaces, and drag-and-drop functionalities, without the need for extensive programming knowledge or experience. This approach accelerates development, reduces costs, and empowers
citizen developers and business users to create custom solutions.

How to learn Low/No Code?

  1. Choose a no/low code platform that suits your needs (popular ones include Bubble, Webflow, and Zapier)
  2. Start building your own projects and experimenting with the platform's features and functionalities.
  3. Iterate and refine your solutions based on feedback and testing.
  4. Join communities or forums to connect with peers, ask questions, share projects, learn from others in the field, and stay up-to-date on new developments.
