What is AR VR?

AR (Augmented Reality) and VR (Virtual Reality) are computer-generated sensory experiences. AR
enhances the real world by overlaying computer generated objects through devices such as smartphones and smart glasses. VR is a fully
immersive experience where users are placed within a virtual environment through a headset. These
technologies are used in industries such as entertainment, education, healthcare, and gaming.
They are also being explored for their potential in training and simulation applications.

How to learn AR VR?

  1. Define your AR/VR learning goals by identifying the desired outcomes and specific skills you wish to develop.
  2. Learn the fundamentals of AR/VR development by starting with the basics of programming and 3D modeling, which are essential skills.
  3. Choose an AR/VR platform that aligns with your learning goals from popular options like Unity, Unreal Engine, and ARKit.
  4. Participate in AR/VR workshops and events to network with developers, learn new techniques, and gain hands-on experience.
  5. Practice building AR/VR projects, starting with simple projects and gradually increasing complexity.
  6. Join online communities or forums to connect with peers, ask questions, share projects, learn from others in the field, and stay up-to-date on new developments.
