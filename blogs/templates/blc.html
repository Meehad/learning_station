Blockchain

-By Irfana

What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a secure digital ledger that uses cryptography to record transactions in a decentralised and transparent way. It enables peer-to-peer
transactions without intermediaries and has applications in various industries, including finance and supply chain management. It also enables
decentralised applications and smart contracts.

How to learn Blockchain?

  1. Learn fundamentals such as cryptography, consensus algorithms, decentralised networks, and smart contracts.
  2. Choose a blockchain platform to specialise in, such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, Hyperledger, or Corda, and get familiar with their specific features, tools, and programming languages.
  3. Learn programming languages commonly used in blockchain development, such as Solidity, Go, Java, and Python.
  4. Practise developing blockchain applications, setting up nodes, and deploying smart contracts on a test network or blockchain sandbox.
  5. Explore real-world blockchain use cases in various industries, such as finance, supply chain, healthcare, and more, to gain insights into how blockchain technology can be applied.
  6. Join communities or forums to connect with peers, ask questions, share, learn from others in the field, and stay up-to-date on new developments.
